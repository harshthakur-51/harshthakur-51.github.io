Jul 2025 - Apr 2026

Low-Noise Amplifier Design using NMOS CVF OTA for Neural Recording

Final Year Thesis | Accepted: NEleX-2026 International Conference on Next Generation Electronics

A low-power neural recording front end designed and simulated in UMC 180 nm CMOS for implantable brain-machine interface use cases.

  • Contributed pseudo-resistor design, gain and bandwidth verification, and comparative literature review across 9+ architectures.
  • Achieved simulated 33 dB gain, 1.84 uW power, and 0.21 uV input-referred noise.
  • Reported 93.63% noise reduction and 45.9% lower power against the compared state-of-the-art design baseline in the thesis study.

Jan 2025 - Oct 2025

Fault Detection in Optical Fibers using OTDR and ANN

IEEE paper | Under review

A lightweight fully connected ANN for automated optical fiber fault classification from OTDR traces.

  • Trained on 11,706 labelled samples spanning 8 fault classes.
  • Achieved 99.96% classification accuracy in the reported experimental setup.
  • Optimized the model direction for near real-time inference and low computational overhead suitable for edge deployment.
